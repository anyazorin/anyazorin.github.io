Dexterous manipulation is a fundamental capability for robotic systems, yet progress has been limited by hardware trade-offs between precision, compactness, strength, and affordability. This work presents RUKA, a tendon-driven humanoid hand that is compact, affordable, and capable. Made from 3D-printed parts and off-the-shelf components, RUKA has 5 fingers with 15 underactuated degrees of freedom enabling diverse human-like grasps. To address control challenges, we learn joint-to-actuator and fingertip-to-actuator models from motion-capture data. Extensive evaluations demonstrate RUKA's superior reachability, durability, and strength compared to other robotic hands.
